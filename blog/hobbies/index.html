When I step away from the rigors of engineering, I seek clarity in the abstract.

You will often find me engaging in dialectics on cosmology and orbital mechanics with fellow aerospace minds, or navigating the strategic complexities of the chessboard. It is a discipline of foresight and patience, a quiet war on 64 squares where I am perpetually refining my command of the game.

Musically, I find resonance in the melancholic. Whenever I am near a guitar, I instinctively return to the fretboard to test the endurance of muscle memory against a three-year hiatus. It is a tactile, analog escape, a necessary counterbalance to a life defined by scientific precision.
