üß™ Research Experience

Prior to collaborating with Prof. Kostas from EPFL, I worked for over two years as a Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru, focusing on the intersection of mechanics, materials, and scientific computing.

At IISc, I began in Prof. Rajesh Chaunsali‚Äôs group, investigating wave control in non-reciprocal spring-damper lattices. I developed MATLAB simulations to study dynamic behavior in odd systems and simultaneously worked on building an active experimental setup using motors. I implemented low-level motor synchronization in Python and C++, enabling real-time modulation of pendulums with programmable springs.

Subsequently, I joined Prof. Akshay Joshi‚Äôs group to work on inverse design of heterogenous non-linear materials. I contributed to the EUCLID framework by developing growth-inspired algorithms for material segmentation and building physics-informed pipelines to infer hyperelastic properties from displacement data. This work integrated FEA, unsupervised learning, and PDE solvers, and was presented at ESMC 2025 in Lyon, France.

My computational foundation was laid during my undergraduate studies at NIT Silchar. I collaborated with Prof. Atanu Sahu on finite element modeling of laminated composite plates under thermal and dynamic loading, later extended to hygrothermal coupling. Prior to that, I worked with Prof. Vishwajit Anand at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) BHU, Varanasi, where I developed MATLAB scripts to estimate dynamic parameters of structures under seismic waves.
