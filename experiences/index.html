Research Experience

I am currently working as a Research Scientist at Vizuara AI Labs, where my research lies at the intersection of Scientific Machine Learning (SciML), physics-informed learning, and computational mechanics. My work focuses on developing scalable learning frameworks for forward and inverse problems in complex physical systems, with an emphasis on tightly coupling data-driven models with governing physical laws.

In parallel, I am collaborating with Prof. Kostas at EPFL on developing a foundational framework for the inverse design of granular architected materials using Generative AI models. This work aims to unify granular physics, metamaterial design, and large-scale generative learning to enable data-efficient discovery of mechanically programmable granular systems.

Prior to this, I spent over two years as a Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, working at the interface of wave physics, materials, machine learning, and scientific computing. 

<p>
  I began my research career in <a href="https://rajeshchaunsali.github.io/">Prof. Rajesh Chaunsali’s group</a>, 
  where I investigated <b>wave control in non-reciprocal spring–damper lattice systems</b>. I developed MATLAB-based 
  numerical algorithms to study non-reciprocal dynamics and simultaneously built an <b>active experimental setup 
  using motor-driven pendulums</b>. I implemented low-level motor synchronization in <b>Python, Arduino, and C++</b>, 
  enabling real-time stiffness modulation using programmable springs.
</p>

<p>
  Subsequently, I joined <a href="https://d3lab-iisc.github.io/">Prof. Akshay Joshi’s group</a> to work on the 
  <b>inverse design of heterogeneous nonlinear materials</b>. I contributed to the <b>EUCLID framework</b> by 
  developing random seed-based material segmentation algorithms and building physics-informed pipelines to infer 
  hyperelastic properties directly from displacement data. This work integrated <b>finite element analysis, 
  unsupervised learning, and PDE solvers</b>, and was presented at the 
  <a href="https://esmc2025.sciencesconf.org/">European Solid Mechanics Conference (ESMC) 2025</a> in Lyon, France.
</p>

My computational foundations were laid during my undergraduate studies at NIT Silchar, where I collaborated with Prof. Atanu Sahu on finite element modeling of laminated composite plates under thermal and dynamic loading, later extended to include hygrothermal coupling.

Prior to that, I worked with Prof. Vishwajit Anand at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) BHU, Varanasi, where I developed MATLAB-based tools for estimating dynamic parameters of structures under seismic excitation.
